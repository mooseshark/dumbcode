What is Dumb Code?
What is Dumb Code?

Dumb Code is learning and learning should be fun. It is the freedom to experiment. No boundries to confine your code to a specific task. Only limiting yourself to what your imagination can dream up. Taking concepts that are unique and interesting and building things that are pointless and stupid with them. Creating applications that serve no purpose. Making things for your own enjoyment. Writing dumb code is a way to expand your knowledge without holding yourself to reasonable meaning behind what you've built. Sometimes you end up with something cool. Sometimes you end up with something bad. But, you'll always end up with something and you'll always learn.

Why is Dumb Code?
Why is Dumb Code?

It's as simple as "I get bored" and as difficult as "I lose interest". I get bored reading tutorials and I lose interest in what I'm studying. I've found the easiest way to combat losing interest is to build something. If I'm building something I'm interested in, I'm more motivated to read and search and deep dive into anything that will help me with what I'm building.

That might not sound dumb at all, but the approach I like to take is often to solve a problem that doesn't really exist. An example would be to find a problem like adding two numbers together, but try to solve it without using the '+' operator. It's an easy problem with an easy solution, but including. that restriction makes it a bit more challenging. With that one restriction I'm now open to other possible ways to solve the problem. Maybe I shift some bits around in binary to create the result, or I figure out some wild algorith that lets me multiply the numbers then divide by some constant to get the intended result. I don't know exactly how I'd solve the problem, but I now have the freedom to pursue a soltion outside the normal expected parameters of the problem. There's nothing to stop me from learning something new, and that new thing I learn could be useful in some other application. If for some reason I can't come up with a solution, it's fine. No new solution needs to exist because using the '+' operator is already the best solution. But, this overcomplication of a simple problem doe make it dumb and the code I write for it won't exectly be smart or useful. That's why I'd call it Dumb Code.

I realize this is a weird approach to learning, but it's one that's helped me take concepts I didn't even remotely understand and really get a solid grip on them. And it's not always the best solution to learning. Sometimes I need the structure and the focus of a normal tutorial.

When is Dumb Code?
When is Dumb Code?

Dumb Code is anytime you feel like it. You don't need a reason to make a dumb app, game, or web page. You just need some free time and an idea. So enjoy these blogs, get ideas, make something unique or pointless or stupid and have fun while you're doing it. You never know what you'll learn!
