Who is Dumb Code?
Who is Dumb Code?

Dumb Code is me. I'm a full time C# developer. My former roles include full stack Sencha ExtJS/ColdFusion project lead, tier 3 tech support, and produce associate. I'm far from the world's best programmer. I probably fall somewhere below the line of "World's OKest Programmer", but I'm pretty good at teaching myself how to do things and I'm hoping that will translate into my blog posts. Maybe I'll even be able to teach someone else something along the way.

What do I do when I'm not coding?
What do I do when I'm not coding?

I like to play video games, collect video games and consoles, repair video game equipment, watch movies, build cars, race cars, build computers, play bass and skateboard. Somehow I've tricked myself into only liking hobbies that are relatively expensive. That makes coding one of least expensive things I like to do. So, if you find that my blog posts don't really help you with programming, I can also teach you to change an alternator, build a gaming PC, or ollie.

Like most people, I also like music. If I'm programming, I'm probably listening to some sort of chiptune. But outside of that, I grew up on punk, fell in love with hip hop, and have an odd affair with nerdcore. I will always recommend you check out Mega Ran because he's the GOAT, but it's important to note listening to Dual Core and YTCracker helped me pass my Security+ exam. So, don't sleep on the nerdcore artists.

Outside of coding, what to expect from blog posts?
Outside of coding, what to expect from blog posts?

Information-wise, I'll probably stick to programming. But, I have been known to dabble in security, hacking, and automation. Working help desk taught me how to automate tasks to make my work easier, and that's what really kicked off my career with programming. So, expect to see some batch and powershell scripting come up at some point. I've also made IT/development my career for the last 10 years. I'm not opposed to sharing experiences that I think may help someone who is just starting out, or trying to advance their career. I'm still trying to advance my career as well, so I'll always be looking at new information to help out with that.

Most importantly, I want to have fun! Blog posts are going to get corny, and weird, and all sorts of referential, but at the end of the day I hope at least one person learns something new from them, even if that one person is myself.
